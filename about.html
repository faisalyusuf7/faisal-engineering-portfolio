About

I like engineering when the screen work has to survive the real world.

My work moves between CAD, simulation, controls, manufacturing, and troubleshooting. That mix is what keeps me honest: elegant geometry still has to print, assemble, move, and explain itself.

Background

UIC mechanical engineering graduate based in Chicago.

I earned a Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Illinois Chicago, graduating in May 2026. My project work includes a five-bar pick-and-place robot, ANSYS thermal-structural analysis, CAD-driven product concepts, and 3D printed prototypes.

Outside pure mechanical design, I also worked as a Computer Specialist at UIC College of Nursing, where I supported hardware deployments, endpoint configuration, networking issues, software support, and maintenance workflows. It is a different kind of engineering muscle: diagnose quickly, communicate clearly, and keep systems reliable for real users.
